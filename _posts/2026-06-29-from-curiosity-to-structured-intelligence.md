# From Curiosity to Structured Intelligence

> "Questions are inexpensive. Reliable answers require architecture."

Welcome to the first entry of my research journal.

This website is not intended to be another collection of disconnected notes or random technical articles. Instead, it serves as a continuously evolving laboratory where ideas are explored, challenged, refined, and documented through structured reasoning.

## Why this site exists

Modern information ecosystems generate overwhelming quantities of data.

The real challenge is no longer finding information.

The challenge is distinguishing:

- facts from assumptions
- evidence from speculation
- correlation from causation
- confidence from correctness

My objective is to build systems that reduce complexity without oversimplifying reality.

---

# Guiding Principles

## Curiosity before certainty

Interesting questions often matter more than immediate answers.

## Evidence before opinion

Claims should be traceable.

Every conclusion should identify:

- supporting evidence
- conflicting evidence
- assumptions
- confidence level

## Systems over isolated facts

Individual facts rarely explain complex phenomena.

Relationships between observations often reveal more than the observations themselves.
