## Working Principles

| Principle | What it means in practice |
|---|---|
| **Follow the evidence** | Claims require support, not confidence alone. |
| **Interrogate assumptions** | Every model contains blind spots until tested. |
| **Prefer reproducibility** | If a conclusion cannot be revisited, it is fragile. |
| **Separate ideas from identity** | A hypothesis is not a personality trait. |
| **Document uncertainty** | Unknowns are not weaknesses; they are boundary markers. |

## Current Focus

### 1. Research systems
Designing workflows that transform raw observations into structured knowledge.

### 2. Adversarial thinking
Testing whether a claim survives pressure, ambiguity, contradiction, and cross-context comparison.

### 3. Knowledge architecture
Building durable notes, taxonomies, and reference chains so ideas do not collapse into a pile of disconnected fragments.

### 4. Frontier questions
Exploring topics that are controversial, underexplained, or poorly modeled by conventional summaries.
